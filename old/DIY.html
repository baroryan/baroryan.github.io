DIY Projects

While I study earthquake cycles, I occasionally enjoy using Arduino coding, soldering, and inexpensive microchips and sensors to develop tools that can be used to create engaging demonstrations or even to study climate change. Here are some DIY projects I designed and built using affordable tools that anyone can acquire online:




Time Lapse from my (previous) LDEO office window



Trying to take advantage of the astonishing four seasons at LDEO, I set up an old smartphone to take pictures from my office window. After two something years and COVID I turned these pictures into timelapses:

I used these tools:



  • Android OnePlus A3010 with 64 GB
  • Tasker app - Used to take a picture every hour
  • AutoInput app - Used to unlock the screen before taking the pictures
  • Foldersync app – Used to upload the pictures to my cloud every night so I don’t need to touch the phone
  • iMovie – Used to edit the pictures into a movie
  • Convert – Used to add text to the pictures



Temperature and Humidity Sensors



Using a few cheap microchips, sensors, Arduino, and MQTT protocol, I built a few sensors that upload their data to an online database accessible online. Use these links to monitor the outdoors temperature at Lamont and my place.

  • Wemos D1 Mini Microchip - Used to control the sensors and upload data to the database using MQTT
  • AHT10 High Precision Digital Temperature and Humidity Sensor - Indoors temperature and humidity sensor
  • DS18B20 Temperature Sensor - Outdoors waterproof temperature sensor
